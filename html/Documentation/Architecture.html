System Architecture

Subsystem Overview

Aggregating and presenting detailed summarizations of reviews consists a lot of computations in the backend. Extracting knowledge from consumer reviews involves complex logic and machine learning. The backend is divided into several subsystems according to their major tasks. The implementation of subsystems is addressed with a modular component approach. This document describes each of the subsystems in the computational backend.

Following diagram shows a process view of the subsystems.



The total system consists of 5 major subsystems

  1. Information Retrieval Subsystem
  2. Ontology/Domain Knowledge
  3. Ontology Based Information Extractor
  4. Sentiment Analyzer
  5. Aggregator

Subsystems 2 to 5 are of major research interest. Subsystems 1,3,4,5 form a computational pipeline. To briefly summarize each of the systems have following tasks. IR sub system is responsible for scanning through ecommerce sites and review sites like amazon , cnet and etc and collecting reviews for products. Once new reviews are collected these are pushed to or pulled from the OBIE subsystem. Before beginning to extract information from the gathered reviews, OBIE system identifies the product category of the review sets given by the IR. Then it check for matches with existing models for the same category. If IR system has retrieved two review sets for the same product model and misidentified them as different products, the OBIE system tries to rectify the mistake.



The OBIE system uses the domain knowledge from the product ontology.

The output from the OBIE system is pushed to the Sentiment analyzer where it uses the identified features and their descriptions to identify the customer opinion for those features. The review aggregator uses the output from the Sentiment analyzer to aggregate the opinions for each feature and it persists the aggregated results as well as the individually analyzed reviews to a persistence store. The frontend uses this persistence store to present users with review aggregations and other useful information.
